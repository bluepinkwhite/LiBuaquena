gender




A person's gender might not always match the one they were assigned at birth.

source: AMAZE org

Sex and Gender
Sex and gender aren't the same thing. Sex is based in biology; it has to do with the body you were born in. Gender is more of a social construct. It's also important to know that neither sex nor gender are binary. Sex isn't just male or femaleâ€”intersex people, whose sex characteristics don't exactly match those of the two categories. As for gender, nonbinary people exist, and their gender goes beyond being just a man or a woman.


Gender Identity and Expression
Gender identity refers to a person's sense of their own gender. Gender expression, on the other hand, is how a person shows their gender to others through physical appearance, names, pronouns, and/or mannerisms. A person's gender expression may not fit your idea of how someone of their identity would present, and that's completely fine!


Transgender
A trans person is someone who does not identify with their sex assigned at birth. This is not only for binary trans people or people who identify as strictly man or woman, but non-binary a people as well.

Nonbinary
A nonbinary (also written as non-binary) person is anyone who does not identify within the male/female binary. This could mean they feel a connection to both, neither, sometimes one or the other, etc.

Genderfluid
Someone who identifies as genderfluid feels that their gender changes over time. For example, they may feel masculine one day, feminine another day, and androgynous the next. It basically means that their gender is "fluid."

Agender
Agender means a "lack" of gender; someone who is agender may feel they are neither man or woman or anything.
