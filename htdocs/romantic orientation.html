romantic orientation




There are different types of attraction, and they may be experienced in different ways.

Attraction
Attraction isn't just about who you're attracted to; it's also about how you experience this attraction, and whether you even do experience it.

As mentioned in the page on sexuality, some people don't experience sexual attraction. This is called asexuality. Similarly, aromantic people don't experience romantic attraction. Just like all other aspects of queer identity, asexuality and aromanticism are spectrums—not everyone will be asexual or aromantic in the same way. There are also plenty of other labels in these spectrums, but of course, there's no pressure to find one that perfectly fits you!


Aromantic
Aromantic people feel little to no romantic attraction to others. They may or may not be interested in romance, as well.

Aromantic Spectrum (Arospec)
The aromantic spectrum isn't just a line with aromanticism on one side and alloromanticism (the opposite; fully experiencing romantic attraction) on the other. There are plenty of different experiences that can't exactly be sorted by how much attraction one feels.

Arospec can also be used as an identity label for anyone who experiences romance and romantic attraction in a nonnormative way! I (Ben :]) have found terms that further specify my aromanticism, but I prefer to use this term for myself. (I also think the flag is pretty)

Demiromantic
For demiromantic people, a close emotional connection is needed before they could ever feel romantic attraction to someone. This isn't just about wanting to get to know someone before dating them—demiromantic people genuinely don't feel romance until they've formed a close bond with someone.

Aegoromantic
Aegoromanticism is about enjoying the idea of romance, but not wanting to actually participate in it. They may enjoy romance in fiction, or when it involves people other than themselves.

Grayromantic
This term describes people who relate to aromanticism, but not completely—a sort of gray area in between aromanticism and alloromanticism. They may feel romantic attraction very weakly, or under unknown circumstances.

Cupioromantic
Cupioromantic people don't feel romantic attraction, however, they enjoy the idea of romance.

Apothiromantic
This is sort of like the opposite of cupioromantic! Apothiromantic people don't feel romantic attraction, and they are repulsed by romance.

Notes!

  • These terms can be paired with others to specify how gender plays a part in your attraction! For example, you can be biromantic and demiromantic, or acespec and a lesbian.
  • Aromantic people are not necessarily asexual too, and the same goes the other way around! There's nothing wrong with being asexual and alloromantic, or aromantic and allosexual.
  • Since asexuality is also a spectrum, all the labels above have sexuality counterparts (e.g., demiromanticism's counterpart is demisexuality)!
  • There are plenty of other less common labels on the aromantic spectrum! AUREA's glossary has a list of identity terms with sources.
